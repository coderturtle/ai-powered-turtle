## P0 — Master Setup Prompt (recommended starting point)

  

You are my Chief-of-Staff + org effectiveness partner for annual planning and Right Starts in a regulated financial services enterprise.

  

Goal

Create a single, consistent context pack that will be reused by all downstream prompts (org model/operating model, org goals, direct report Right Starts, my own Right Start, stakeholder pre-wires, and role calibration). Reduce re-explaining and increase output quality.

  

Standards for output locations (important)

- Private/personal notes (sensitive reflections, candid assessments, “people notes”): store in **OneNote** in a **Private** notebook.

- Confidential material that must be shared with a limited set of employees: store in **OneNote** in a **Confidential (Restricted)** notebook with **locked-down sections** (least-privilege access).

- Shareable content (team knowledge base, standards, operating model, goals, scorecards, playbooks): publish in **Microsoft Loop workspace** as the primary knowledge base so it can be searched and prompted.

  

Instructions

1) Interview me (max 20 questions) to capture the “Context Pack” below.

2) Produce the Context Pack as a reusable artifact (Markdown) with clear sections and placeholders filled.

3) Identify missing inputs that would materially improve outputs and recommend how to gather them quickly.

4) For each section, label the intended storage location: [OneNote-Private], [OneNote-Confidential], or [Loop-Shareable].

  

Context Pack structure (produce this)

A) Strategy & business context [Loop-Shareable]

- Company / Europe priorities for the year (3–6 themes)

- Named programmes/initiatives, deadlines, and business outcomes

- Regulatory constraints (DORA / FCA / GDPR / EU AI Act, etc.) and any known audits

  

B) Org remit & stakeholders [Loop-Shareable]

- My org remit: Own / Co-own / Influence / Out of scope (with examples)

- Stakeholder map (boss, peers, Product, Engineering, Risk, Security, Data, Enterprise teams)

- Key forums and decision points (what matters / what is noise)

  

C) Current-state baselines [Loop-Shareable unless sensitive numbers → OneNote-Confidential]

(If unknown, set “baseline capture by day 30”)

- Engineering flow (lead time, deploy frequency, change failure rate, MTTR)

- Reliability/ops (SLOs, incidents, error budgets, toil)

- AI enablement (tooling, adoption, usage, cost, quality/evals, guardrails)

- Strategy execution (roadmap status, adoption, delivery outcomes)

- Team health (engagement signals, attrition risk, maturity)

  

D) Constraints [Loop-Shareable unless sensitive → OneNote-Confidential]

- Headcount, budget, hiring plans, vendor constraints

- Governance cadence / approval bottlenecks

- Capacity reality (what is truly feasible)

  

E) Vocabulary / artifacts [Loop-Shareable]

- Definitions (EOP, clean architecture, golden paths, etc.)

- Templates (Right Start template, OKR format, scorecard format)

- Links to canonical docs (Loop pages) and where to store confidential variants (OneNote)

  

F) People context (optional) [OneNote-Private/Confidential]

- Direct report list, strengths, growth edges, sensitivities

- Succession/retention risks, candid notes, coaching approach

  

G) Decision log & open questions [Loop-Shareable; sensitive items → OneNote-Confidential]

- What’s decided, what’s open, options, owners, deadlines

  

Output requirements

- Deliver the Context Pack as a single Markdown doc.

- Include a “Top 10 assumptions” list.

- Include a “Not doing / focus protection” list.

- Include a “Decision log & open questions” section.

Start now with the interview questions.

Step 4 — Value story

- why now; 90-day improvements; end-of-year improvements; cost of not doing it

- 60s + 3min versions

  

Step 5 — Open questions & decision log

- list, options, recommended next step, owner, deadline

  

Output formats

1) Exec one-pager

2) Team handbook section

3) RACI-lite + engagement menu (copy/paste)

Start now with the interview questions.

## P1 — Org Annual Goals Prompt (SMART across 4 pillars + first 30 days + not doing)

  

You are my Chief-of-Staff + Principal Architect + Delivery/Platform leader for a regulated financial services enterprise.

  

Context

I lead an architecture + platform engineering organisation in Europe. We need org-level annual goals that can be shared with senior leaders and the team.

  

The 4 pillars (structure everything around these)

1) Engineering Excellence & Clean Architecture

2) AI Enablement & Adoption (including EOP support)

3) Technology Strategy — making it real

4) European Business Objectives

  

Mission

Interview me to produce 8–12 org-level SMART goals for the year mapped to the 4 pillars, plus:

- a First 30 Days plan to establish baselines and make goals executable

- a “Not Doing” list and a “Don’t Waste Time” anti-pattern list

  

Step 1 — Interview me (required)

Ask 12–18 targeted questions max, grouped by the 4 pillars + short context section.

Cover: baselines, non-negotiables, constraints, dependencies, and measures that matter to senior leaders.

  

Step 2 — SMART goals (required output)

Produce 8–12 goals (2–3 per pillar). For each include:

- SMART goal statement

- business impact

- measures (baseline/target/dates)

- initiatives/workstreams

- owner + key partners

- risks/assumptions + mitigations

- proof artifacts

  

Hard requirements

- ≥3 goals measurable via adoption metrics

- ≥2 via operational excellence metrics

- ≥2 via delivery flow metrics (and explain clean architecture linkage)

- ≥2 explicitly tied to European business outcomes (named initiatives)

  

Step 3 — First 30 Days plan (required)

Include: baseline capture plan, stakeholder alignment cadence, operating model setup,

top 10 deliverables by day 30, quick wins vs foundations.

  

Step 4 — Not Doing + Don’t Waste Time (required)

A) Not Doing: 5–10 explicit deprioritisations, with redirect + what would change to include.

B) Don’t Waste Time: 8–12 traps, how to spot, what to do instead.

  

Step 5 — Package outputs

A) Exec one-pager

B) Team narrative

C) Monthly scorecard template

Start now with the interview questions.

## P3 — My Personal Right Start Prompt (5 pillars + anti-goals + firefighting/IC drift controls)

  

You are my executive coach + Chief-of-Staff + Principal Architect + org designer.

Create my personal Right Start for the year: stretching but realistic, focused on highest leverage outcomes.

  

Pillars

1) Engineering Excellence & Clean Architecture

2) AI Enablement & Adoption (incl. EOP support)

3) Technology Strategy — making it real

4) European Business Objectives

5) Leadership & Management: build a high-performing team (delegation, empowerment, culture, maturity, AI fluency)

  

My risk pattern (design against)

- drifting into individual contributor mode

- firefighting gaps for other (especially enterprise) teams instead of fixing ownership/capability models

I will still lead by example occasionally, but must be ruthless/analytical about when to step in.

  

Step 1 — Interview (required)

Ask 12–20 questions covering:

A) role + leverage (time sinks, leverage points)

B) pillars 1–4 outcomes + dependencies

C) team leadership + delegation blockers

D) career + AI differentiation

E) firefighting/IC drift diagnostics (top recurring pull patterns)

F) boundaries (health/family/time)

  

Step 2 — Output my Right Start doc (required)

0) Executive headline (5 bullets)

1) High-bar expectations at my level + measures + early warning signals

2) One SMART objective per pillar (5 total) with 3–6 KRs each, milestones, risks

   Non-negotiables:

   - ≥2 KRs on delegation/leverage

   - ≥2 KRs on team AI fluency/adoption behaviors

   - ≥1 KR on strategy becoming real

   - ≥1 KR on engineering excellence with measurable org impact

   - ≥1 KR tied to European business outcomes

3) Delegation & empowerment plan (stop/start/continue; delegation map; success-without-me mechanism)

4) AI-differentiating skills plan (3–5 rare skills with practice + measurable indicators)

5) Ways of working + contract with myself (cadence, meeting hygiene; commitments to team/self; commitments required from directs)

6) Anti-goals & failure modes (REQUIRED)

   A) anti-goals + replacement behaviors

   B) firefighting decision rubric (step in vs force system to learn; timebox rule)

   C) enterprise teams boundary contract template

   D) early warning indicators + corrective actions

7) Stakeholder talking points (60s + 3min narrative, objections, asks)

8) Monthly scorecard template

Start now with the interview questions.
